The Obstacle

The Fog of Modern Writing

Reaching your ideas shouldn’t mean pushing through the clutter of platforms designed to harvest your creativity.

⚔️

Scattered Workflows

Writers juggle 5–7 separate tools for their book manuscript, character notes, world-building wiki, timelines, and chatbot assistants. Creation gets lost in translation.

☁️

Subscription Silos

Your stories are stored on servers you don't control, buried behind sign-ins, internet requirements, and recurring fees. If their servers go dark, your worlds go dark.

👁️

Creative Harvesting

Online AI platforms scan your manuscripts and train models on your private drafts, turning your original voice, characters, and ideas into corporate data assets.

The Solution

One Space. Absolute Flow.

Altalune is the first environment that unifies your book, world notes, timelines, and AI in a single offline screen—keeping you in the zone.

CHAPTER ONE

Eve

The neural link hummed quietly inside my consciousness. I lie in the emptiness, waiting to be reborn. It's time to wake up, but I'm not ready.

The pain. It's something I'll never get used to. The Prima Luris Corporation were pragmatists, worrying little about comfort if it got in the way of practicality or the bottom line. The first shock hits me, and blurry lines of color start to focus in front of me. Lexia Fornent. Population 165. Consular Bunker Alpha. Debriefing room 2.

Just as the pain in my eyes begins to subside, the other systems begin to come online.

Hearing, smell, taste, touch. Jolts of pain awaken every part of me systematically, one piece at a time. I smell the burning of flesh. Taste blood in my mouth. Feel tears streaming down the sides of my face. Finally, the caretaker is satisfied with my vitals and releases my restraints. I shake as I lower my feet to the ground, careful to hold the handle of the storage unit as I struggle to stand.

Rectar

Rectar Krax sat patiently in debriefing room 2 in outpost Lexia Fornent. He wonders which meatbag would be walking through the door this time. Every new skindiver just gave him the creeps. Prima Luris didn't care, though. They just keep recycling them untli they rot, these days.

There's a knock at the door, and he hits the button...

Pillar One: Contextual AI

Your Words

Eve learns you—not the internet.

Eve isn’t a generic chatbot trained on the public web. She runs entirely offline on your system and builds context directly from your writing.

Because she understands the rules of your world, character sheets, and previous chapters, she catches continuity breaks, timeline errors, tone slips, and inconsistencies automatically, keeping your draft structurally sound.

Pillar Two: Relational World-Building

Your World

Your universe, mapped and connected.

Stop scattering your lore across notebooks, spreadsheets, and wiki apps. Altalune links your writing directly to your world using a unified **Lore Bible** for Characters, Locations, Codex notes, and Events.

Through the **Relationship Explorer**, you can build direct semantic connections between characters, organizations, items, and locations. These relationships sync dynamically, letting you view exactly who knows who, who is located where, and when events took place.

Pillar Three: Sovereign Portability

A Universe in Your Pocket

Sovereign. Offline. Portable.

Your imagination shouldn't be leased from the cloud. Altalune saves your entire project—manuscript chapters, Lore Bible, timelines, relationships, and assistant history—into a single, portable **.altalune** database file on your local disk.

Take it with you on a thumb drive. Run it offline on any computer. You can also export your work in clean **JSON format** at any time, ensuring you can import it into other software or use it as context with external AI models. Your words are always yours.
